Steak with mash, mushrooms, and potato balls

Description

This recipe is a homemade steak dish. It is seared in tallow and butter, then served with mashed potatoes and mushrooms. It also includes potato balls.

Ingredients

Steak

  • 25g of Beef Tallow.
  • Tablespoon of Butter.
  • Your choice of Rump Steak.
  • Salt and Pepper.

Mash

  • 500g of potatoes, peeled and cut in half.
  • 50ml whole milk.
  • Two tablespoons of butter.

Mushrooms

  • Tablespoon of butter.
  • A dash of extra virgin olive oil.
  • 500g of chopped mushrooms.
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced.

Potato Balls

  • 100g of potato balls/pops.

Steps

Steak

  1. Remove steak from packaging, place onto a plate and salt and pepper liberally. Leave to stand at room temperature for half an hour.
  2. Pat dry with paper towel, salt and pepper again. Heat a cast iron pan on a high heat.
  3. When the pan starts smoking, add beef tallow and butter -It should melt almost instantly. Place your steak in the pan.
  4. I advise 2 minutes a side for medium rare, 2 and a half for medium, and 3 for well done.
  5. Remove steak from the pan and let it rest for 5 minutes. I find wrapping it in tinfoil with garlic and butter to be delicious.
  6. Serve

Mash

  1. Peel and chop 500g of potatoes, then place into boiling water.
  2. Boil potatoes until you can stick a knife in them with no resistance, then drain and place back into the pot.
  3. Add your milk and butter, and start mashing the potatoes. If you want them creamier, feel free to add more.
  4. When there are no lumps, Serve.

Mushrooms

  1. Chop 500g of Mushrooms, then place into a hot frying pan.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil, then fry with your added garlic cloves.
  3. When the mushrooms are your desired doneness, Serve.

Potato Balls

  1. Pour 100g of potato balls into an air fryer or oven, then cook for 15 minutes at 200c.
  2. Serve.
